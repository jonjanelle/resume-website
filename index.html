Bio

click to begin.

Thinking about how to solvie technical, and ideally mathematical, problems has always been a deeply rewarding experience for me.

I began learning about software development in 2001 when I took my first high school independent study course in computer science using C++. As an undergraduate I was excited to continue learning about mathematics and computer science, but discovered that I also loved serving others as a mentor after working as a teaching assistant in an introductory RISC assembly language course. This lead me to finish my undergraduate degree in mathematics education and enter a career as a high school mathematics teacher where I taught AP Calculus AB and AP Calculus BC in central Maine for two years.

During my first two years of teaching I became increasingly curious about mathematical modeling and data science, and this lead me to spend substantial amounts of time exploring the R and Python languages. I began by completing eight courses in data science and statistics offered by John Hopkins University via Coursera, which gave me a strong foundation in statistical techniques and R programming. Concurrently, I began using Python to create create graphs for classroom use and to create simulations in the SageMath software system.
